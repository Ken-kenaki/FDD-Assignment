Mindfulness for Stress: 10 Minutes

Downshift the nervous system with a simple, guided practice that fits a busy day.

Aug 15, 2025 Dr. Maya, PsyD 7 min read

Set Up

Sit comfortably, upright but relaxed. Place feet on the floor, rest hands on thighs, and soften the jaw and shoulders.

Guided 10‑Minute Flow

  • 1–2 min: feel the contact points—feet, seat, back.
  • 2–6 min: follow the breath; count 1–5 on inhales, 1–5 on exhales.
  • 6–8 min: when the mind wanders, silently note “thinking,” and return.
  • 8–10 min: widen awareness to sounds and bodily sensations together.

For Busy Days

Micro‑sessions work: 60–180 seconds between tasks. Pair with a cue, like closing a laptop or standing after a call.

Sleep Carryover

Body scanning and lengthened exhalations before bed reduce arousal and make sleep onset easier—especially after stressful evenings.

Tip: set a daily 10‑minute calendar block; treat it like a meeting with future self.
