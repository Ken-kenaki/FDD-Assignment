Beginner Strength: A Safe, 3‑Day Plan

Build strength and confidence with a minimal, repeatable routine focused on form and recovery.

Aug 20, 2025 Coach Rene CSCS 9 min read
text

Safety First

Warm up 5–8 minutes, move through full ranges with control, and stop a rep or two before failure (RPE 6–8) while learning form.

Three‑Day Template

  • Day A: Goblet squat, push‑up or incline push‑up, one‑arm row.
  • Day B: Hip hinge (RDL), overhead press, lat pull‑down or assisted pull‑up.
  • Day C: Split squat, chest press, plank and side plank.

Start with 2–3 sets of 6–10 reps; add a rep each session or small load weekly when reps feel strong.

Warmup & Cooldown

Dynamic prep: 2 sets of 8–10 reps of bodyweight squats, hip hinges, band rows, and scap push‑ups. Finish with easy walking and gentle stretches.

Simple Progress Log

Keep a small notebook or notes app: exercise, load, reps, RPE. Review weekly and nudge one variable upward at a time.

Reminder: soreness isn’t the goal—quality reps and consistency are.
