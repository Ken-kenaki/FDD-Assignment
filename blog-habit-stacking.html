Habit Stacking: Tiny Steps, Big Wins

Attach small actions to routines already happening to make change effortless and durable.

Aug 10, 2025 Coach Amir 5 min read

The Formula

After I current routine, I will new tiny habit. Keep it small enough to do even on tough days, then celebrate with a quick “good job” to reinforce the loop.

4 Rules

  • Obvious: place cues where the action happens.
  • Attractive: pair with a pleasant activity like music or coffee.
  • Easy: reduce friction—1 minute to start is enough.
  • Satisfying: track streaks; tiny wins compound.

Examples

  • After brushing teeth, do 10 slow squats.
  • After making coffee, write 1 line in a gratitude note.
  • After lunch, 5‑minute walk outside or stair laps.

Two‑Week Plan

Week 1: pick 2 anchors and 2 tiny habits. Week 2: keep those and add one more. Protect consistency over intensity; scale only after habits feel automatic.

Template: After I [routine], I will [tiny habit] for [1–2 minutes].
